# Getting Started

Welcome to Kaivue. This guide walks you through your very first login — from finding your Recording Server to watching your first live camera feed.

## What you need

- The Kaivue mobile app installed on your phone (iOS or Android), **or** a modern browser on your computer (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
- A username and password (or an SSO account) provided by your administrator
- Your phone or computer on the same local network as your Recording Server, or access through your organization's VPN / public URL

---

## Step 1: Discover your Recording Server

Your Kaivue Recording Server (also called a "Directory") is the device or server that stores your camera footage and manages access. There are three ways to find it:

### Option A: Automatic discovery (recommended on local networks)

When you open the Kaivue app on your phone while connected to the same Wi-Fi network as your Recording Server, the app scans automatically using mDNS (a technology similar to how your phone finds printers). Any nearby Kaivue servers appear in a list — tap the one you want to connect to.

![App showing a list of auto-discovered Recording Servers on the local network](./images/placeholder-discovery-list.png)

### Option B: QR code

Your administrator may have printed or emailed a QR code that encodes your server's address. In the app, tap **Scan QR code** and point your camera at it. The connection is configured automatically.

![QR code scanning screen in the Kaivue app](./images/placeholder-qr-scan.png)

### Option C: Enter the address manually

If you're connecting from outside your local network (for example, through a VPN or a public domain name), tap **Enter address manually** and type the hostname or IP address your administrator gave you. It typically looks like `nvr.yourcompany.com` or `192.168.1.100`.

![Manual address entry screen](./images/placeholder-manual-address.png)

---

## Step 2: Log in

Once the app connects to your Recording Server, the login screen appears.

### Local account login

Type your username and password, then tap **Sign In**.

![Login form with username and password fields](./images/placeholder-login-form.png)

<Tip>
  If you forgot your password, tap **Forgot password?** and follow the reset instructions. If you don't see that option, contact your administrator — password reset may be managed by your organization.
</Tip>

### SSO login (Single Sign-On)

If your organization uses a company identity provider (like Microsoft Entra, Okta, or Google Workspace), tap **Sign in with [your organization]** instead. You'll be redirected to your organization's familiar login page, then returned to Kaivue automatically.

![SSO login button on the login screen](./images/placeholder-sso-button.png)

---

## Step 3: Verify you can see a camera

After you log in, you land on the camera list (or a dashboard your administrator has configured). You should see at least one camera listed.

Tap any camera name to open its live feed.

![Home screen showing a list of cameras with status indicators](./images/placeholder-camera-list.png)

If the stream loads and you see a live image, you're all set. If something goes wrong, check the [troubleshooting guide](./troubleshooting).

The only difference is that some cloud-based features (like remote access without a VPN) may not be available. Your administrator can tell you which access method applies.
</Note>
