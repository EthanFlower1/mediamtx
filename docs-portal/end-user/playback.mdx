# Playback

<Warning>
  **Coming in Kaivue v1.x** — Playback with a timeline scrubber is not yet available. This page describes how it will work when released.
</Warning>

Playback lets you review recorded footage from any camera. Jump to any moment in the past, scrub through video, bookmark key moments, and export short clips.

---

## Opening playback

From the live view screen, tap the **clock icon** or swipe up to reveal the timeline. You can also open playback directly from your camera list by pressing and holding a camera, then choosing **View recording**.

![Playback screen with timeline scrubber at the bottom](./images/placeholder-playback-timeline.png)

---

## Jumping to a specific time

The timeline at the bottom of the screen shows recorded segments as colored blocks (blue = continuous recording, yellow = motion-triggered clips, grey = no recording).

Ways to navigate:

| Action | What it does |
|---|---|
| Tap a point on the timeline | Jump to that moment |
| Drag the timeline left or right | Scrub forward or backward |
| Pinch the timeline | Zoom in (more detail) or zoom out (wider view) |
| Tap the time display | Type an exact date and time to jump to |

---

## Playback controls

Once you're in a recorded segment, standard controls appear:

- **Play / Pause** — start or stop playback
- **Speed** — play back at 0.5x, 1x, 2x, or 4x speed
- **Previous / Next event** — jump to the nearest motion or alert event
- **Go live** — jump back to the current live view

---

## Bookmarks

Tap the **bookmark icon** while watching a recording to save that moment. Give it a name (optional) — for example, "Suspicious vehicle" or "Delivery at 3:12pm". Your bookmarks are private to your account unless you share them.

To review bookmarks, tap **Bookmarks** in the playback screen's top toolbar.

![Bookmarks list showing saved moments with timestamps and names](./images/placeholder-bookmarks-list.png)

---

## Exporting a clip

To save a short video clip:

1. Tap **Export** (the share icon) while in playback.
2. Drag the handles on the timeline to set the start and end of your clip.
3. Tap **Export clip**. The clip is processed on the Recording Server and downloaded to your device.

<Info>
  Clip length may be limited by your administrator (for example, a maximum of 10 minutes per export). If your selection is too long, you'll see a message telling you the maximum allowed duration.
</Info>

Exported clips are saved in MP4 format and include the camera name, timestamp, and a watermark (if your administrator has enabled one).

---

## How long is footage kept?

Your administrator sets a retention period for each camera — for example, 30 days or 90 days. Footage older than that is deleted automatically. You can see how far back a camera's recording goes by looking at the left edge of the timeline.

---

## When this ships

Playback is tracked for the Kaivue v1.x release. Until then, if you need to retrieve specific footage urgently, contact your administrator — they have access to recordings through the admin interface.
