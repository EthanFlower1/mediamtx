# Privacy and Your Data

This page explains what information Kaivue stores about you as an end user — as distinct from camera footage, which is covered by your organization's own privacy and retention policy.

---

## What Kaivue stores about you

### Account data

When your administrator creates your account, Kaivue stores:

- Your name, email address, and username
- A hashed (not readable) version of your password if you use a local account; if you use SSO, no password is stored
- Your role and permissions within the system
- The date your account was created and last modified

### Session and access data

Every time you log in, Kaivue records:

- The time and date of the login
- The IP address of the device you logged in from
- The device type (mobile or browser) and approximate browser/OS version
- Whether login succeeded or failed

This information is part of the audit log, which helps your administrator detect unauthorized access.

### Viewing activity

Kaivue records which cameras you opened and when. This is also part of the audit log. Your administrator and their own administrators can see this history. It cannot be hidden.

<Note>
  This viewing history exists to meet compliance requirements (such as SOC 2 and FedRAMP). If your organization is subject to regulatory oversight, this audit trail may be required. Talk to your administrator or your organization's privacy officer if you have concerns.
</Note>

### Push notification tokens

If you enable push notifications (once available in v1.x), Kaivue stores a device push token — an identifier your phone's operating system provides — to send notifications to your specific device. This token does not identify you personally and changes periodically.

### Bookmarks and notes

Any bookmarks or notes you add to recordings are stored on the Recording Server associated with your account.

---

## What Kaivue does NOT store about you

- Your location (GPS coordinates are never collected)
- The contents of your talkback audio sessions beyond the live stream
- Any biometric data about you as a viewer (face recognition, if enabled, applies to people detected on camera — not to you as a user of the app)
- Browsing activity outside the Kaivue app

---

## Who can see your data

| Data type | Who can see it |
|---|---|
| Your account details (name, email) | Your administrator |
| Your login history | Your administrator |
| Your camera viewing history | Your administrator |
| Your bookmarks | Only you (and your administrator if they access the database directly) |
| Camera footage | Your administrator and anyone they grant access to |

Kaivue does not sell or share your personal data with third parties. Your integrator (the company that deployed Kaivue for your organization) may have their own data handling practices — consult your organization's privacy notice.

---

## Face recognition opt-out

If face recognition is enabled on any cameras you are observed by, you have the right to opt out of having your face enrolled in any watchlist. To request this:

1. Contact your administrator and state that you are requesting a face recognition opt-out.
2. Your administrator can remove any enrollment of your face from watchlists in the admin interface.
3. You may also request confirmation that no image of your face is stored as a reference template.

Face recognition is disabled by default on all cameras and must be explicitly enabled by your administrator on a per-camera basis.

## Your rights (GDPR and similar regulations)

If you are in a jurisdiction with personal data protection laws (such as the EU's GDPR, the UK's UK GDPR, or California's CCPA), you may have the following rights:

- **Right of access** — request a copy of the personal data Kaivue holds about you
- **Right to rectification** — request correction of inaccurate data
- **Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")** — request deletion of your personal data

### How to request data deletion

1. Contact your administrator (the person who manages your Kaivue account) or your organization's designated privacy officer.
2. State that you are making a data erasure request under applicable privacy law.
3. Your administrator can delete your account and associated personal data (name, email, login history, bookmarks) from the Kaivue system.

<Warning>
  Deleting your account removes your access to Kaivue permanently. Your viewing activity in the audit log may be retained in anonymized or aggregated form if required for compliance purposes — your administrator can clarify your organization's retention policy.
</Warning>

Camera footage containing images of you is governed by your organization's video retention policy, not your individual account. For questions about that footage, contact your organization's privacy officer directly.

---

## Security of your data

Kaivue stores your password as a one-way cryptographic hash (bcrypt). It cannot be reversed to reveal your original password. Communication between your device and the Recording Server is encrypted using TLS.

If you believe your account has been accessed without your permission, contact your administrator immediately. They can review the login audit log and revoke active sessions.
