# Provider Obligations

**Article:** 16
**Status:** Draft — cross-checked against SOC 2 control library KAI-385

## Purpose

Art. 16 enumerates the obligations of providers of high-risk AI systems. This document maps every Art. 16 obligation to the Kaivue artefact that discharges it, so auditors have a single point of entry.

## Refusal at the product level

Several Kaivue product rules enforce provider obligations through code:

- **Art. 5 refusals.** The face-recognition configuration surface refuses to enable:
  - Real-time live detection in EU regions until legal review clarifies Art. 5(1)(h) scope.
  - Biometric categorisation inferring any of the Art. 5(1)(g) sensitive attributes. (Kaivue does not ship biometric categorisation at v1 at all.)
  - Emotion recognition in workplaces or education. (Not shipped at v1 at all.)
- **Model-provenance refusals.** The inference stack refuses to load any model that fails the sha256 or signature check.
- **Tenant-boundary refusals.** The API layer refuses any cross-tenant operation via Casbin policies.
- **Unapproved-model refusals.** The inference stack refuses to load any model not in `approved` state in the model registry (KAI-279).

## Field corrective action procedure

Art. 20 requires providers that become aware of non-conformity to take corrective actions and inform the distributors, deployers, authorised representatives, and importers. Kaivue's procedure:

1. **Detection.** Non-conformity is detected via `post-market-monitoring.md` or via incident intake (`serious-incident-reporting.md`).
2. **Assessment.** lead-security and lead-ai jointly assess scope (which tenants, which Member States, which models affected).
3. **Immediate mitigation.** Rollback, disable, or isolate as appropriate.
4. **Customer notification.** Affected customers are notified via the contractual incident-notification channel with a plain-language description, the mitigation steps they should take, and the corrective action Kaivue is rolling out.
5. **Authority notification.** Where the issue rises to Art. 73 reporting, `serious-incident-reporting.md` is followed. Where it does not but Art. 20 still applies, a notification is filed with the relevant market surveillance authorities via the designated channels.
6. **Fix.** The root-cause fix flows through the normal release process, including the promotion gates (`fairness-testing-protocol.md`, accuracy/robustness validation).
7. **Verification.** Post-fix verification confirms the non-conformity is resolved across all affected deployments.
8. **Record.** The incident record is retained for 10 years.

## Relationship with authorised representatives (Art. 22)

Where Kaivue is not established in the Union, Art. 22 requires appointing an authorised representative in a Member State. The representative is named in the declaration of conformity and on the trust portal, and holds a copy of this compliance package for regulator access.

## Relationship with deployers (customers)

The customer agreement reflects the provider/deployer split:

- Kaivue as provider bears the Art. 16 obligations in this document.
- Customer as deployer bears the Art. 26 obligations summarised in `transparency-and-information.md`.
- Where a customer uploads their own model via KAI-291 and materially modifies the system, the customer assumes provider obligations for that model under Art. 25. Kaivue retains platform-provider obligations. This is documented in the custom-model upload acceptance terms.
